A sociology course is designed to provide students with a deep understanding of societal structures, human behavior, and the impact of social forces. The curriculum covers various sociological theories, research methods, and the analysis of social issues. Students engage in studying topics such as social inequality, culture, institutions, and global perspectives. The program encourages critical thinking, sociological imagination, and the ability to analyze and interpret social phenomena. Through a combination of theoretical study and practical research, students develop insights into the complexities of societies and cultures.
